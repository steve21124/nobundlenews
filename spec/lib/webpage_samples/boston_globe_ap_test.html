Maine bill promotes solar power

May 28, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • |
Text size – +

AUGUSTA, Maine—A bill aimed at protecting Mainers' right to install solar devices and clotheslines where they live has gained ground in the Legislature.

After a debate Thursday, the House voted 76-65 in favor of the bill. Portland Democratic Rep. Jon Hinck's bill would restrict regulations or bylaws that bar renters or condominium owners from setting up energy-saving solar units and clothes lines.

Supporters said the bill would help Mainers save money by not wasting energy, and said the time is right for the bill. Opponents maintained the bill is impractical and violates apartment owners' rights.

The bill faces further House and Senate votes.
